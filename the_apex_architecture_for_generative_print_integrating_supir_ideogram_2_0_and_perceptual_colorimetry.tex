\section{Measure-Theoretic Typographic Bounding}
Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mu)$ be a measure space representing the continuous 2D canvas of the poster, where $\mu$ is the Lebesgue measure. Let $T_i \subset \Omega$ be the spatial bounding box for the $i$-th typographic element. 
\begin{definition}
A layout distribution $\mathcal{L}$ is valid if $\mu(T_i \cap T_j) = 0$ for all $i \neq j$ and the text density $\rho(T_i) \ge \delta$ for some threshold $\delta > 0$.
\end{definition}
Ideogram 2.0 and AnyText 2 operate by conditioning the score-matching diffusion process. We formulate bounding box optimization as minimizing the Wasserstein distance $W_2(p_{data}, p_\theta)$ subject to the constraint that the support of the generated text measure is restricted to $\cup_{i} T_i$. By employing Regional Prompter, we inject cross-attention masks $M_i(x,y) = \mathbb{I}_{\{(x,y) \in T_i\}}$, forcing the self-attention matrices to obey $\int_{T_i^c} A(x,y) d\mu(y) = 0$.

\section{Persistent Homology in SUPIR Upscaling}

\begin{theorem}
Let $X$ be the base low-resolution image manifold and $Y$ be the upscaled high-resolution manifold. The SUPIR injection preserves the Betti numbers $\beta_k(X)$ up to a threshold $\epsilon$.
\end{theorem}

\section{Perceptual Colorimetry and CMYK Mapping}
Let $\mathcal{C}_{RGB} \subset \mathbb{R}^3$ and $\mathcal{C}_{CMYK} \subset \mathbb{R}^4$ be the source and destination color gamuts respectively. 
\begin{lemma}
The mapping $\Phi: \mathcal{C}_{RGB} \to \mathcal{C}_{CMYK}$ under Perceptual rendering intent with Black Point Compensation is a volume-preserving diffeomorphism modulo the gamut boundary.
\end{lemma}
